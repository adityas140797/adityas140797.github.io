About Us


Jolt India™ is a Gurugram based start-up that intends to provide its customers a comfortable ride to their destination by supporting the Indian Government’s vision to promote the electric vehicles. Deepak Kalra the brain behind Jolt India™ started the services for college students, so that they could themselves drive the electric vehicle to their favourite hotspots, after a positive feedback from the students the company decided to provide the services to the residents of the national capital with the main objective to tackle the problem of pollution in the national capital.

Statement of the problem

1. State the main problem you seek to address: The proposed project intends at solving the existing problem of pollution by conventional vehicles and traffic congestion by conventional electric vehicles. According to a survey conducted by IIT Delhi in 2015, two wheeler vehicles contribute about 33% of the total PM10 particles in the National Capital.

2. Who has this problem, where does it occur? There are around 2.27 people affected by pollution in the Delhi-NCR region. The problem of pollution is common around the world; millions of people are affected from it.

3. How did you come to know of this, did the people who have problem approach you or you visualized it yourself? The problem of pollution is rising at an alarming rate in the country especially in the national capital, New Delhi. The problem is getting worse year by year .The International Energy Agency expects that demand for energy from transport for India would increase to 174 Million Tonnes oil equivalent by 2030 (IEA, 2013), nearly 3 times from 2011, this would lead to an substantial increase in the pollution level.

4. Why is it important to solve it? It’s not only important, but the urgent to report and rectify this problem of CO2 emissions. Currently, transport sector is one of the major contributors of CO2 emission and as per 2012–13 data; the transport sector is responsible for 14% of India’s energy-related CO2 emissions. An assessment carried out by Central Pollution Control Board surveyed that 75% cities are at very high risk of PM10 levels. Out of these, 50% of cities have critical level of NOx as well.
